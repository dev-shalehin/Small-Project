Learning UI/UX design requires a mix of theory, practice, and real-world examples. Start by understanding the basic principles of design such as color theory, typography, spacing, and visual hierarchy. Then move on to UX fundamentals like user research, wireframing, prototyping, and usability testing.

You can use tools like Figma or Adobe XD to practice designing interfaces. Most importantly, analyze popular apps and websites to understand why certain design choices work well for users.
